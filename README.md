# Chrono-Scientist: Bones of Meaning

Chrono-Scientist: Bones of Meaning is an educational game developed in Unity as part of my Master's thesis in Computer Science at the University of Bern.

The project brings together game development, Human-Computer Interaction, educational technology, anatomy, and archaeology into a single interactive learning experience designed for children.

---

## Project Overview

The game is designed for children aged 8–12 and focuses on learning through interaction, exploration, and gameplay.

Players travel through different historical and evolutionary periods using a fictional device called the Chrono Engine. Throughout the journey, they complete anatomy-inspired challenges, interact with side characters, and gradually discover how bones connect movement, history, and scientific understanding.

The project is heavily inspired by museum-style learning environments and aims to make educational content feel interactive rather than purely instructional.

## Research Focus

This thesis explores how interactive digital systems can support interdisciplinary learning for younger audiences.

The research combines technical development with user experience and educational evaluation, with a particular interest in:
- engagement through gameplay
- interaction design for children
- museum-inspired digital learning
- educational storytelling
- user-centered and system-centered evaluation methods
